The red panda is a small, tree-dwelling mammal found in the eastern Himalayas and southwestern China. Despite its name, it is not closely related to the giant panda. Red pandas have reddish-brown fur, a long, bushy tail, and a waddling walk due to their short front legs. They are primarily herbivores, feeding on bamboo, fruits, and berries. Red pandas are shy and mostly active at night. Habitat destruction poses a serious threat to their survival.
