The Bengal tiger is one of the largest and most well-known tiger subspecies. It inhabits India, Bangladesh, Nepal, and Bhutan. This predator is known for its bright orange fur with black stripes. Tigers are solitary hunters and can travel long distances in search of prey. Their diet mainly consists of deer, wild boar, and other large herbivores. Unfortunately, due to poaching and habitat loss, the Bengal tiger population is decreasing and is now classified as endangered.
